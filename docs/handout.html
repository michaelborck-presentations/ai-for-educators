AI for Educators: Handout

Prompts and Techniques for Thoughtful AI Use

Faculty Development | Dr. Michael Borck | 2026

The Billboard Rule

Before sharing anything with AI, ask: "Would I be comfortable if this appeared on a billboard?" Never share student data, personal information about others, or confidential institutional data. Always verify AI outputs - remember Deloitte's $440,000 mistake.

The Core Insight

At the heart of ALL AI are instructions. The clearer your instructions, the better the output. This is sometimes called "prompt engineering" or "context engineering" - but really, it's just giving clear, specific instructions.

Board of Directors Strategy

Get multiple expert perspectives on a decision or plan in one prompt. Replace [YOUR SITUATION] with your actual context.

Multi-Perspective Analysis

I'm [YOUR SITUATION - e.g., redesigning an assessment for my management unit].

Act as my advisory board and analyse this from multiple perspectives:

1. As Learning Designer: Alignment with learning outcomes?
2. As Academic Integrity Officer: AI vulnerability and integrity?
3. As Student Advocate: Workload, clarity, and fairness?
4. As Industry Partner: Real-world relevance and skills?

For each perspective, give me 3-4 key considerations.

Try These Situations

  • Redesigning an assessment that's vulnerable to AI
  • Creating an AI use policy for your unit
  • Planning a new module or course
  • Developing feedback approaches for large classes

Devil's Advocate Technique

Force AI to argue against your position to find weaknesses. Replace [YOUR POSITION] with what you want to stress-test.

Critical Analysis

I believe that [YOUR POSITION - e.g., banning AI from assessments is the best approach for maintaining academic integrity].

Play devil's advocate. Give me the strongest possible arguments against this position.
Be harsh but fair. I want to know:

1. Why might this approach fail?
2. What am I probably not thinking about?
3. Who would this NOT work for?
4. What unintended consequences could occur?

Don't hold back - I need to hear the hard truths.

Key Phrases That Work

"Be harsh but fair" and "Don't hold back" give AI permission to be genuinely critical instead of polite. Without these phrases, AI tends to soften its critique.

Reverse Prompting

Let AI interview YOU before giving advice. This produces much more tailored recommendations than generic queries.

AI Interviews You

I want to [YOUR GOAL - e.g., create an AI use policy for my business management units].

Before giving me any advice, interview me first. Ask me questions one at a time
to understand:
- My teaching context and student level
- My current assessment types
- My concerns about AI use
- My goals for student learning
- Any constraints I'm working with

After you understand my situation, then give me tailored recommendations.

Start with your first question.

Use Cases for Educators

  • Creating AI policies tailored to your context
  • Redesigning assessments for AI resilience
  • Developing rubrics for new assessment types
  • Planning curriculum changes

Assessment Stress-Test

Run your assessment through AI to see how vulnerable it is. This reveals what was already breakable.

Test Your Assessment

Here is an assessment task I give to students:

[PASTE YOUR ASSESSMENT BRIEF HERE]

Please complete this assessment as a student would, using only the information
in the brief and your general knowledge.

After completing it, tell me:
1. How well could AI complete this independently?
2. What aspects require genuine student thinking?
3. How might I redesign this to make student judgment more visible?

Three Redesign Options

Option A - Process Documentation: Submit AI transcript, annotate errors, reflection on improvement

Option B - In-Class Components: AI draft before class, in-class critique, live presentation

Option C - Personal Application: Local context, primary data, unique perspective AI can't have

Bonus: Teaching Support

Practical prompts for everyday teaching tasks.

Generate Feedback (then edit it)

I'm providing feedback on a student essay for [UNIT/TOPIC].

The assignment asked students to [ASSIGNMENT REQUIREMENT].

Here is the student's work:

[PASTE STUDENT WORK]

Draft constructive feedback that:
1. Identifies 2-3 strengths
2. Identifies 2-3 areas for improvement with specific suggestions
3. Ends with an encouraging next step
4. Is appropriate for [YEAR LEVEL] students

Note: I will review and personalise this before sending to the student.

Generate Discussion Questions

I'm teaching [TOPIC] to [YEAR LEVEL] [DISCIPLINE] students.

Generate 5 discussion questions that:
1. Move beyond recall to analysis and application
2. Connect theory to real-world business scenarios
3. Could spark genuine debate (not just "right answers")
4. Are appropriate for a 50-minute tutorial

Include one "devil's advocate" question that challenges conventional thinking.

Verification Checklist

Always verify AI outputs before using them. Remember: AI is confident even when wrong.

  • Can I find cited sources independently? (Search for them)
  • Do statistics appear in other reputable sources?
  • Does this make logical sense based on what I know?
  • Would I stake my professional reputation on this being accurate?
  • Have I reviewed and personalised the output (not just copied it)?

Your One Commitment

Start small. Scale what works. Share with colleagues.

  • Use AI to draft feedback on student work (then edit it)
  • Try the Board of Directors prompt on something you're planning
  • Run one of your assessments through AI to see what happens
  • Use Reverse Prompting when you're unsure where to start
